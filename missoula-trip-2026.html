Wild Facts About Our Flight!
Things to blow your mind at 35,000 feet
🚀
We're going FASTER than a bullet
A plane at cruising speed (~575 mph) is about 2× faster than a typical bullet from a pistol. You're basically a superhero.
🏔️
We fly OVER the Rockies
Mount Elbert, the tallest peak in the Rockies, is 14,440 ft tall. We cruise at 35,000 ft — that's more than 2× its height, above us!
☁️
We're above the clouds
Most clouds form below 20,000 ft. At 35,000 ft we're way above them — which is why it's always sunny up there even on a rainy day below!
🗺️
We cross 4 states!
Georgia → Tennessee/Alabama → over the Great Plains → Wyoming/Montana → Idaho → Washington. That's a road trip that would take 3 days in under 6 hours!

What to Expect — April in Missoula
  • Daytime highs in the 50s–low 60s°F — comfortable with a light jacket
  • Nights drop to the mid-30s — bring a real coat for mornings and evenings
  • Layering is key — temps can swing 20+ degrees in a day
  • The Clark Fork is running high and cold — beautiful to look at from a safe distance
  • Spring wildflowers starting to pop on the hillsides
  • Mount Sentinel still likely has snow near the M
  • Rattlesnake Wilderness trails may be muddy from snowmelt

Cool Things To Do in Missoula

20 ideas — you all know this place better than any list, but here's some inspiration for the week

1
🌊 Watch Brennan's Wave
The standing wave on the Clark Fork right downtown — in April with snowmelt the river is absolutely ripping. Watch kayakers throw tricks while the kids lose their minds.
OutdoorKidsPeak in April
2
🥾 Hike the M on Mount Sentinel
The classic Missoula hike. Steep enough to earn it, short enough to do with the whole crew. Views of the valley from the top are as good as it gets — and there's probably still snow up top in April.
OutdoorKidsLocal Staple
3
🎠 Caras Park & the Carousel
The hand-carved Carousel for Missoula is genuinely one of the coolest things in town and it never gets old with kids. Caras Park right on the river is a perfect spot to hang out, especially on a nice spring day.
KidsOutdoorLocal Staple
4
🍦 Big Dipper Ice Cream
Non-negotiable. The Big Dipper is a Missoula institution and seasonal flavors in April are always a treat. Even if it's 45 degrees — that's what Montana people do.
FoodKidsLocal Staple
5
🚶 Kim Williams Trail
The riverside trail that runs along the Clark Fork toward the canyon — flat, beautiful, and the river in April is wild. Easy enough for grandparents and little ones, long enough to actually feel like a walk.
OutdoorKidsPeak in April
6
⛷️ Snowbowl — Spring Skiing
Early April at Snowbowl often means spring corn snow and cheap lift tickets with zero lift lines. Worth a call to check conditions — a morning ski run with the fam would be hard to beat.
OutdoorApril DependentLocal Secret
7
💦 Splash Montana
Indoor waterpark — basically a guaranteed hit with five kids regardless of weather. Perfect backup plan for a rainy April 8th or whenever the crew needs to burn some energy.
KidsLocal Favorite
8
🏔️ Blue Mountain Recreation Area
Less trafficked than Rattlesnake, great ridge views of the whole Missoula valley. A solid family hike that feels like you're actually getting into the mountains, not just the hills.
OutdoorLocal Favorite
9
🐾 Rattlesnake Wilderness
Classic Missoula backyard wilderness. The lower trails near the creek are accessible and beautiful in April — snowmelt waterfalls, early spring greening, and you can go as deep or as easy as the crew wants.
OutdoorKidsLocal Staple
10
👻 Garnet Ghost Town
About 45 minutes out on gravel roads — an old gold mining ghost town that's genuinely well-preserved and fascinates kids every time. Check road conditions in April (can still be snowy/muddy) but worth it if passable.
Day TripKidsCheck Road Conditions
11
🎨 Missoula Art Museum
Free, right downtown, and genuinely good. Rotating exhibits lean heavily Montana and Western contemporary — worth a wander on a rainy afternoon, especially with the older kids.
CultureLocal Gem
12
🪂 Smokejumper Visitor Center
The national base for aerial firefighters — tours of the facility are surprisingly riveting and the history is incredible. The kids will be amazed, and honestly so will the adults. Free tours when in season.
CultureKidsHidden Gem
13
☕ Hip Strip Hang (Higgins Ave)
Walk the Hip Strip from the bridge south — coffee at Black Coffee Roasting or Plonk, browse Rockin Rudy's for a record or quirky gift, check out Shakespeare & Co. Pure Missoula energy.
Local VibeCoffee & Shops
14
🍺 KettleHouse Brewing
Cold Smoke Scotch Ale is basically Montana in a glass. Both taprooms are welcoming — the North Side location has outdoor space. A pint with your dad while the kids run around outside is a solid afternoon.
AdultsMissoula IconFood & Drink
15
🏛️ University of Montana Campus Walk
The UM campus is beautiful in early spring — stroll the Oval, check out the Urey Lecture Hall area, and look back at Mount Sentinel from the lawn. If you've got roots there, show the kids what Montana higher ed looks like.
OutdoorLocal Pride
16
🌿 Pattee Canyon
The local's picnic and trail spot — less crowded, beautiful ponderosa pines, and easy trails that work for everyone from grandparents to little kids. Good place to bring a lunch and just be in the woods.
OutdoorKidsLocal Favorite
17
🚗 Bitterroot Valley Drive
Head south on 93 into the Bitterroot — Stevensville, Hamilton, the Sapphire Mountains rising to the east and the Bitterroots to the west. April means big sky and snow-capped peaks still fully loaded. Classic Montana road.
Scenic DriveOutdoor
18
🏔️ Drive to Alberton Gorge
30 minutes west on I-90 — the Clark Fork cuts through a dramatic canyon. In April it's running massive from snowmelt. Pull off at the rafting put-ins and watch the water. The kids will be floored by how powerful it is.
Short DriveOutdoorPeak in April
19
🎳 Family Game Night / Board Games
Eight people, two generations of kids, one house — this will happen at least once and it's always the best. Missoula has great local game shops if you need something new. The real activity is just being in the same room.
KidsFamily Time
20
🌅 Just… sit on the porch
You flew across the country to see your parents, your sister, and her kids. Sometimes the best thing to do in Missoula is exactly nothing — coffee in hand, mountains in view, everyone in the same place. That's the whole point.
